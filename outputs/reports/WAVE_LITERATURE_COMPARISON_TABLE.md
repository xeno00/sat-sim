# Wave Literature Comparison Table

- Status: non-final qualitative comparison.
- Safe framing: JCLS provides a cooperative mechanism for resolving position-clock ambiguity without relying on batching, reference receivers, or terrestrial anchors.
- Unsafe framing: JCLS beats Starlink PNT or other literature rows under non-comparable assumptions.

| Method | Signals | GNSS assistance | Cooperation | Clock correction assumed | Position error | Citation | Caveat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| JCLS wave diagnostic | NTN downlink TOA plus UE sidelink TOA | No GNSS assumed in estimator state | UE cooperation via sidelinks | Joint UE and non-reference satellite clock-offset estimation relative to reference satellite | Computed in this branch; non-final | This diagnostic branch | Not manuscript-ready; cannot be compared directly to field trials. |
| Starlink opportunistic carrier-phase PNT | Ku-band Starlink signals of opportunity, carrier phase/Doppler style tracking | Often requires known ephemeris/receiver setup assumptions; GNSS-denied motivation | No UE cooperation | Receiver/satellite signal structure handled by opportunistic receiver processing | Reported examples include meter-level to tens-of-meters field results | [Kassas et al., first Starlink carrier-phase tracking and positioning results](https://ece.osu.edu/sites/default/files/2022-09/The_first_carrier_phase_tracking_and_positioning_results_with_Starlink_LEO_satellite_Signals.pdf) | Different signal, receiver, and batching assumptions; do not frame as head-to-head. |
